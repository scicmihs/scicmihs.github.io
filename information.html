Adjudicators

General Effect Music: John White (Click to Expand Bio)

John White was a member of the Plantation High School Band, the 1980-84 Festival of States National Field Champions. He was a charter member of the Florida Wave Drum & Bugle Corps from 1981-82, and he performed as a member of the City of Sunrise Jazz Ensemble from 1983 to 1998. He is a graduate of Florida Atlantic University in Boca Raton, Florida. Mr. White created, arranged for, and performed with a brass quintet for the Florida Renaissance Festival from 1993 to 2009. In 1999 he joined the low brass staff of the Boston Crusaders in the year they broke into the DCI top 12. Mr. White began his teaching career as a high school orchestra director and guitar teacher, then in 2006 opened Markham Woods Middle School in Lake Mary, Florida, as its first band director. In 2017, he began teaching for the Seminole County Virtual School, teaching middle and high school guitar, and developing classes in music appreciation, music theory, bass guitar, and instrumental techniques for wind instruments. He is a founding director of the Lake Mary-Longwood Summer Band Camp. Mr. White has served as an adjudicator for the Central Florida Private School Solo and Ensemble Association since 2010, he and has been a member of the Central States Judges Association since 2008. He has provided comments and feedback to many area schools as they prepare for their annual performance evaluations. Mr. White is an Elder at Calvary Chapel of the Lakes in Umatilla, Florida, and also serves as the IT/Media Ministry leader there. He and his wife and daughter currently live near Ocala, Florida.

Ensemble Music: Phil Rodell (Click to Expand Bio)

Musician, drill writer and choreographer Phil Rodell has been involved with indoor and outdoor pageantry arts for over 30 years. After receiving his Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Music from Florida Atlantic University, Mr. Rodell went on tour performing in the original cast of the Tony & Emmy Award winning show Blast. He has taught and marched in several drum & bugle corps and BOA bands, including the Crossmen, Florida Wave, Suncoast Sound, the Star of Indiana Brass Theater, and the 1994 World Champion Blue Devils. Mr. Rodell is also a professional trombonist, performing in area bands and orchestras in the Central Florida area. When not performing, he is adjudicating and teaching marching bands all over the country.

Visual Performance: Patrick Duncan (Click to Expand Bio)

Patrick Duncan began his drum corps career at the age of 16 as a member of the 2003 Spirit of Jacksonville State University Drum and Bugle Corps. He marched as a trumpet player for Spirit in 2003, 2004, and 2005 before moving to The Cadets Drum and Bugle Corps for the 2006, 2007, and 2008 seasons, serving as section leader his final year. As a marching member, Patrick was a decorated member of The Cadets, earning the honors of being named Visual Rookie of the Year in 2006, Brass Distinguished Service in 2007, and Visual and Brass Cadet of the Year in 2008. Patrick has spent time teaching The Cadets, The Boston Crusaders, and is currently on staff with Carolina Crown.  Patrick served as the Director of Bands for Wiregrass Ranch High School in Wesley Chapel, Florida from 2013-2017 and as the Director of Bands for Park Vista Community High School from 2017-2021. His bands at Park Vista appeared at the 2018 Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, the 2019 Bands of America Grand National Championships, placing 16th overall, and numerous competitions and festivals throughout the state. Patrick is currently a real estate agent with Keller Williams Innovations in Lake Worth, Florida. Along with his real estate duties, he designs for bands in Florida, Texas, Alabama, Georgia, and Mississippi. Patrick is an alumnus of Auburn University and The University of South Florida.

Ensemble Visual: Mike Phillips (Click to Expand Bio)

Michael Phillips has participated in the pageantry arts for 45 years. He has filled many roles over the years such as performer, instructor, clinician, drill designer, program coordinator, director and judge. Mr. Phillips has been involved with notable organizations including the Cavaliers, and many other accomplished drum and bugle corps and marching bands at the high school and college levels. Mr. Phillips has been judging for many years in the US and internationally. Mr. Phillips provides clinics internationally to band directors addressing show design, judging methodologies and participation in international competitions. Michael considers it to be a great privilege and responsibility to be a judge. Mr. Phillips realizes the importance of providing positive feedback to band students and directors to help grow their programs and provide an educational and rewarding experience. Michael pledges his very best, impartial judging effort in return for the best seat in the house to watch some of the greatest youth in the world do amazing things on the field of competition.

Color Guard/Auxiliary: Kevin Griner (Click to Expand Bio)

Kevin studied Business Management and Marketing at The University of North Florida. He has served as an entertainment performer and stage manager for the Walt Disney Company for over 24 years. He toured as a Disney Entertainer and worked for 12 months on board the Big Red Boat representing Disney. Kevin wrote and created the Auxiliary workshop “Auxiliration” for Disney's Magic Music Days, in addition to hiring and training all color guard clinicians for the workshop. He is a color guard adjudicator for Heritage Festivals, OrlandoFest, and Festival Disney Music Competitions. Kevin is also a color guard adjudicator for the Florida Bandmasters Association and a winter guard equipment judge for the Florida Federation of Judges Association as well as a Fall marching band color guard and GE judge for several states. Kevin has served 13 years as Color Guard Director for the University of Central Florida Marching Knights and 10 years as director of the award winning WGI Pegasus Winter guard World program. He is a sought-after color guard Director and Designer for numerous outstanding high school visual programs. He was a member of the Bronze Medal Winning Alliance of Miami Winter Guard in 1991 as well as a twenty-five year member and instructor in Winter Guard International and Drum Corps International.​

Percussion: Bryant Bernal (Click to Expand Bio)

Bryant is currently the Drumline Director & Arranger for the UCF Marching Knights. In addition to his duties at UCF, he is the Percussion Director at Hagerty High School and an active percussion educator, arranger, and clinician throughout Florida. His private students have performed in All-County and All-State honor bands, solo and ensemble festivals, DCI, WGI, as well as national and international summer music festivals.

Timing and Penalty: Joe Cavallaro (Click to Expand Bio)

Joe started in the marching arts in 1983 as a marching member of the Patriots Drum & Bugle Corps and continued through 1996, including stops with the Crusaders, USCGA Windjammers and DCA Champion Empire Statesmen. In addition to performing with the famous Jammitors at Walt Disney World and Tokyo Disneyland, Joe taught percussion and visual for high school bands including Hilton HS, East Lyme HS, and the Williamsport Millionaires. He has been a visual judge with CSJA for the past ten years and is a current member of the contest staffs for WGI, the FFCC and FMBC.
